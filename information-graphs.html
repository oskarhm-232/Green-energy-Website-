Data Journalism: Solar Trends in Australia

Local Impact: Solar in Newcastle

Over 1 million homes in NSW have now adopted solar technology. In Newcastle specifically, we have seen a significant surge in rooftop installations. This data demonstrates a clear shift in how our community thinks about energy.

  1. Market Leading: Australia is one of the highest users of solar in the world, with more than 3 million systems installed nationwide.
  2. Newcastle's Contribution: Local suburbs like Merewether, Adamstown, and Wallsend are leading the way in the Hunter region.
  3. Capacity Growth: As shown in the table below, NSW is one of the top performing states with over 8,500 MW of installed capacity.

How do the panels actually work?

Solar panels are like big windows that catch sunlight. Inside the panels, there are special "cells" that turn light into electricity. Even if it is a bit cloudy in Newcastle, they still work! The best part is that once you buy them, the sunlight they catch is 100% free. Modern panels are built to be very tough, so they can handle the rain and wind for over 25 years.

Using power at night

Usually, solar panels only work when the sun is up. But if you get a battery, you can "save" the extra power you made during the day and use it at night. This means you don't have to buy as much power from the big energy companies. It's like having a giant rechargeable battery for your whole house, making sure you always have lights on even when it's dark outside.

Getting help with the cost

Buying solar panels can be expensive at first, but the government wants to help. They have programs that give you a "rebate," which is basically a big discount to make it cheaper. In NSW, there are also special rules that say energy companies have to pay you if your panels make more power than you can use! This helps the panels pay for themselves much faster.
